Workshop on Gromacs & Advanced Simulation Methods

Max Planck Institute for biophysical chemistry. September 24-26, 2008.



Background

We are happy to announce the second special Gromacs workshop together with Helmut Grubmueller & Bert de Groot at the Max Planck Institute for Biophysical Chemistry in Goettingen, Germany. As in the previous workshop at Stanford Universiy, the scope of this workshop is to gather people working on new or otherwise advanced simulations algorithms, either in- or outside Gromacs, but that we believe could be interesting to support in the future. In contrast to the previous tutorials given e.g. in Helsinki, we will assume that the participants here are fairly comfortable with Gromacs, or at least MD simulations in general. We are planning three full days with a mix of formal talks, birds-of-a-feather sessions, hands-on tutorials and question-and-answer sessions in smaller groups. The main Gromacs developers plan to be present.

Topics covered

There are a couple of interesting topics that we have already selected:
  • The new domain decomposition parallelization in Gromacs 4.0, with some tips & tricks to get the most out of your hardware.
  • MD of very large systems
  • Non-Equilibrium MD/Force probe simulations
  • Validation of simulation by experiment and vice versa
  • QM/MM. How do you mix Quantum Mechanics with Gromacs?
  • Virtual sites for hydrogen motion removal and long time-steps
  • Membrane protein simulations
  • Replica exchange
  • Gromacs source code walk-through

However, these are just examples. We would be particularly interested in participants who would like to contribute a small talk (anywhere from 15 to 30 mins) on their favorite subject, or if you have special requests about topics you'd like to learn more about.
